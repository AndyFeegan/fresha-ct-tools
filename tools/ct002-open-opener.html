O

Orient

Name, company, context in under 5 seconds

P

Pattern Interrupt

Break autopilot with an observation, not a pitch

Warning: If the hook isn't real, you've burned trust in the first ten seconds. Only use what's true.
E

Earn Time

Give them a reason to stay — relevant to them, not your features

Tip: Time-boundary technique: "I'll keep this to 30 seconds. If it doesn't sound relevant, I'll let you go." Giving them permission to hang up reduces resistance.
N

Navigate

Ask a question or propose a next step — hand them control

Tip: The question should be easy to say yes to. If you don't Navigate, you monologue — and they're waiting for the moment to say no.
X

Three Failure Modes to Avoid

Apologising: "Sorry to bother you", "I know you're busy" — signals low-value.
Fake permission: "Is now a bad time?" — gives them a free exit with no conversation.
Credential dump: "We work with ten thousand salons", "We're Fresha" — they don't care yet.
